How to Stay Active During the Winter Months

  1. Home
  2. Pages
  3. Blog

Staying active in winter isn’t always easy, especially when it’s cold, dark, and icy outside. But here’s the thing—staying active in winter doesn’t just keep you in shape; it also enhances your mood, boosts your energy, and aids you fend off those winter blues. Whether you love cozy indoor workouts or adventurous outdoor winter activities, there’s something for everyone to stay fit during the colder months.

At Family Physiotherapy, we encourage our clients to prioritize winter physical activity as a way to stay healthy and maintain joint and muscle strength. Plus, if you’re recovering from an injury or have specific fitness goals, our physiotherapy in Edmonton provides tailored guidance to keep you moving safely.

Why Staying Active in Winter is Important?

Winter is often associated with hibernation, but staying active during these months is essential for several reasons:

  • Boost Your Mood: Physical activity boosts the release of endorphins, your body’s natural mood boosters. Staying active can help combat (SAD) seasonal affective disorder and keep those winter blues at bay
  • Maintain Mobility:Cold weather can make your muscles and joints feel stiffer. Regular winter physical activity helps keep your body flexible and reduces the risk of aches and pains.
  • Support Immune Health: Exercise strengthens your immune system, which is especially important during the colder months when colds and flu are more common.

    • Winter Fitness Tips to Keep You Moving

  • 1.Take Advantage of Indoor Workouts

    • Don’t feel like braving the cold? No problem! Winter is the perfect time to explore the world of indoor exercise. You don’t need a gym membership to get moving—there are plenty of options you can do from the comfort of your home.

    Try These Indoor Workouts:

    • Perform Pilates to improve flexibility and core strength.
    • Bodyweight exercises like squats, lunges, and push-ups.
    • Resistance band workouts for strength and mobility.
  • How It Helps: Indoor workouts are an optimal path to stay consistent with your fitness routine without worrying about icy sidewalks or freezing temperatures.

    • 2.Bundle Up for Cold Weather Exercise

    If you love being outdoors, winter doesn’t have to stop you. With the right gear and a little preparation, you can enjoy the fresh air and stay active even in freezing temperatures.

    Tips for Cold Weather Exercise:

    • Wear layers to keep warm while avoiding overheating.
    • Wear moisture-wicking clothes to keep sweat away from your skin.
    • Protect your extremities with gloves, a hat, and thermal socks.
    • Warm up indoors before heading outside to prevent muscle stiffness.

    Outdoor Winter Activities to Try:

    • Walking or jogging in a local park (watch out for icy spots!).
    • Cross-country skiing or snowshoeing for a fun full-body workout.
    • Building a snowman or shovelling snow—yes, it counts as exercise!

    3. Make Winter Physical Activity Fun

    Staying active in winter doesn’t have to feel like a chore. The key is to find activities you enjoy so they feel less like “work” and more like fun.

    Ideas for Active Winter Fun:

    • Ice skating at a local rink.
    • Sledding with your family (the uphill walk is great cardio!).
    • Playing winter sports like hockey or curling.
    • Hosting a winter scavenger hunt with friends.
  • How It Helps:Fun activities keep you motivated and make it easier to stick to a regular fitness routine.

    • 4. Stick to a Winter Workout Schedule

    Staying consistent is essential for fitness success, even during the winter months. Creating a workout schedule can aid you in staying on track and making physical activity a regular part of your routine.

    Tips to Stay Consistent:

    • Set realistic fitness goals for the season.
    • Mix indoor and outdoor workouts to keep things interesting.
    • Find an accountability partner to keep you motivated.
    • Reward yourself for hitting milestones—it could be as simple as a warm cup of tea after your workout!

    How Physiotherapy Supports Your Winter Fitness Goals

    Winter can be tough on your body if you’re dealing with stiff joints, past injuries, or chronic pain. Our physiotherapists provide personalized treatment plans to help you stay active, recover safely, and prevent injuries during the winter months.

    What We Offer:

    • Tailored exercise programs to fit your goals and abilities.
    • Guidance on safe cold-weather exercise and warm-up techniques.
    • Support for managing joint pain, stiffness, or mobility issues.
    • Tips for improving strength, balance, and flexibility throughout the season.

    By working with our physiotherapists, you can stay healthy and make the most of your winter workouts, no matter the weather.
