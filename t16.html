    Vertigo is frequently caused by the following:

    Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV)

    Vertigo is most frequently caused by this. It occurs when the microscopic calcium crystals in your ears become dislodged and migrate to areas of the ear they are unwanted. This can result in unexpected spinning sensations (vertigo) and imbalance in the inner ear as well as your overall health. We provide vertigo treatment to treat BPPV and other forms of vertigo.

    Meniere’s syndrome

    This illness develops when fluid accumulates in one or both ears. This is frequently accompanied by “ringing” in the ear and brief bouts of acute dizziness that may last many hours. Additionally, you may experience brief hearing loss.

    Migraines

    Migraine headaches can also affect the vestibular system, resulting in vertigo episodes that may be accompanied by a sensitivity to light or sound. Additionally, vision may be compromised.

    Vestibular neuritis

    This is an infection of the inner ear that can result in vertigo.

    Stroke

    A stroke is a type of neurological illness that impairs mobility throughout the body. If you’ve just had a stroke, you may have waves of vertigo that last an extended period of time.
