Recognize neck pain:

Neck pain can arise for a variety of causes. Neck discomfort often radiates from the base of the skull to the top of the shoulder blades before settling at the back of the neck. The intensity of the pain varies, ranging from a persistent dull aching to acute, stabbing aches. Neck discomfort may occasionally be accompanied by additional symptoms, including but not limited to the following:

  • Upper body stiffness and muscular tightness
  • Upper extremity numbness or tingling
  • Headaches
  • Discomfort and pain associated with prolonged standing in the same position
  • Arm weakness
  • Sleep deprivation as a result of pain and discomfort
  • Inability to stand fully upright or sit straight

    • The following conditions frequently cause neck pain:

    Neck pain is most frequently caused by an injury to the neck’s tissues or bone structures. However, certain degenerative disorders can also result in neck pain. Several common conditions that cause neck pain include the following:

    Injuries: Whiplash, which is often caused by an automobile or high-impact sports collision, is one of the most prevalent neck discomfort injuries. This is referred to as a “strain” injury because it causes the head to whip rapidly back and forth, injuring the neck’s soft tissues.

    Illnesses: Neck pain can be caused by various diseases and conditions, including rheumatoid arthritis, meningitis, and cancer.

    Compression of the nerves: If you have a herniated disc in the neck or a narrowing of the foramen (a place where the nerves exit the spine), the nerves that branch off from the spinal cord can become squeezed. In certain instances, nerve discomfort can extend all the way from the neck to the hands and fingers – a disorder called “radiculopathy.”

    Strains of muscle: Overuse of the neck muscles can cause them to become strained. Even simple activities such as sitting at your desk all day with your neck craned toward the computer screen can cause neck muscular pain. Click here to know how to get rid of neck muscle pain.

    Degeneration of the joints: As with every other joint in the body, the neck joints can deteriorate over time, resulting in pain. Osteoarthritis, for example, can erode the cartilage between the vertebrae in the neck, resulting in neck pain. Neck Pain Physiotherapy Edmonton can help you get rid of Neck pain.
