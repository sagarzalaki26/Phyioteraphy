What types of injuries are sustained in an automobile accident?

Typically, the most significant motor vehicle accident injury sustained in a car accident are the head and back. Concussions, visual impairments, and skull fractures are all possible outcomes of head injuries. You may have dizziness and headaches following a concussion. A herniated disk is another common automobile accident injury. Neck and chest injuries are also common in accidents, with whiplash being the most common.

How might physiotherapy assist you following a vehicle accident?

You may have discomfort, decreased range of motion, headaches, or stiffness following a car accident. There are numerous reasons to seek physiotherapy following a car accident. It aids in the recovery process and may also help prevent long-term harm from the accident, such as migraines and chronic pain.

Bear in mind that not all symptoms will manifest immediately following a car collision. Therapeutic care enables you to manage pain sensations, enhance flexibility and strength, and recover more quickly. The sooner you receive physiotherapy following a vehicle accident, the more likely you are to live pain-free for the rest of your life. Indeed, a Medscape study discovered that those who did not receive physiotherapy were significantly more likely to develop degenerative disc disease.
