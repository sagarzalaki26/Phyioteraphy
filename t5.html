Managing chronic pain properly using therapeutic care from Family Physiotherapy.

Are you afflicted with chronic pain that impairs your physical abilities? Chronic Pain Physiotherapy Edmonton can help you achieve chronic pain alleviation, even if you have previously attempted physiotherapy treatment.

Pain does not always act in predictable ways. It can sometimes last for days, weeks, or even months as a result of an underlying ailment. When discomfort lasts three months or longer, it is referred to as “chronic pain.” Chronic pain is debilitating on both a physical and emotional level. Family Physiotherapy Edmonton understands your needs and has successfully treated numerous patients suffering from chronic pain. Contact us, let us do the same for you today!

How did I become afflicted with chronic pain?

In the majority of cases, chronic pain has a neurophysiological basis. Chronic injury, disease, anxiety, and depression are all entangled in a convoluted web, causing your neurological system to remain on high alert at all times. Chronic pain can manifest itself in a variety of ways, including back pain, joint pain, nerve pain, and headaches. It is most frequently caused by one of the following;

Accidents:

Any injury, whether in an automobile, on the job, or even in your own house, can result in chronic pain. According to a 2016 OSHA estimate, around 2.9 out of every 100 employees sustain occupational injuries. Furthermore, according to a study published in the journal Pain, 21% of patients engaged in motor vehicle accidents had widespread chronic pain in the weeks after the collision. While this type of discomfort does not subside on its own, seeking the assistance of an Edmonton physiotherapist can help reduce it.

Injuries Due to Excessive Use:

Overuse injuries are quite prevalent, and they can affect any portion of the body that is repeatedly utilized in daily life. For instance, the manner in which you stand, walk, work, and exercise can all have an effect on your body. If an adequate technique is not used, it leads to overuse injuries. This might result in joint and muscle degeneration, resulting in persistent discomfort. Also, if you conduct repetitive heavy lifting at work with inappropriate technique, this can result in persistent back discomfort. Family Physiotherapy can assist you in learning how to move your body appropriately in daily life in order to avoid these problems.

Disease:

Numerous disorders can result in persistent pain, including arthritis, fibromyalgia, multiple sclerosis, shingles, and diabetic neuropathy. All of these factors can lead to chronic pain in any part of your body, which can be eased with physiotherapy treatment.

Surgery:

Surgical techniques are occasionally necessary. While they may be necessary to alleviate some diseases, the recovery period following surgery can be just as uncomfortable. While the surgical restoration of an area of the body is sufficient to remedy a structural problem, we frequently compensate for our pain through our movement. Weeks, months, or even years of consistent movement frequently result in extra pain following surgery. Family Physiotherapy also provides post-surgical rehabilitation therapies that can assist in expediting healing, relieving pain, and reducing the risk of future chronic pain.

How is chronic pain distinguished from acute pain?

The difference between chronic and acute is described below.

Acute pain is characterized as discomfort that resolves spontaneously within a few hours, days, or weeks. A slight ankle sprain, for example, would be called acute pain, as the pain will decrease within a few days.

Chronic pain is a state of persistent suffering that can last three to six months or more. Chronic pain can occur for various reasons, and it is the most prevalent reason for seeking medical attention. It can significantly impair your quality of life by reducing your movement, increasing your reliance on prescription medications, and possibly contributing to anxiety and despair. However, all of this can be avoided with the assistance of Family Physiotherapy Therapists.

Is it possible to find relief from my persistent pain?

Physiotherapy session aims to alleviate pain by identifying the source of the discomfort and treating it directly rather than concealing symptoms with medication.

Our Edmonton physiotherapists are highly trained movement analysts who assess and treat physical disorders as well as their underlying structural causes. Our physical examination will search for vulnerable parts of your body, restricted range of motion areas, and gait or standing abnormalities. This all aids in determining the underlying causes of your persistent pain.

Following the assessment, our physiotherapists will develop a treatment plan tailored to your unique needs. This may involve specialized manual therapy techniques to aid in the restoration of soft tissue and joint movement, as well as pain-relieving therapies and light-focused exercises to increase your strength. Your treatment plan will include both in-clinic and at-home activities to keep you healthy and active at all times.
